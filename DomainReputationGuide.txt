SECTION 1: OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is Domain Reputation?
--------------------------
Domain reputation is a measure of how trustworthy and reliable an email sending
domain is, as perceived by Internet Service Providers (ISPs), spam filters, and
email clients like Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo.

A domain with a strong reputation ensures that emails land in the recipient's
inbox. A domain with a poor reputation results in emails being filtered to spam,
blocked entirely, or rejected outright.

Why Domain Reputation Matters at Zeta Global
---------------------------------------------
Zeta Global manages large-scale email marketing campaigns for hundreds of clients.
A single domain with poor reputation can:
  - Cause thousands of legitimate emails to land in spam
  - Reduce campaign open rates and revenue
  - Damage client trust and relationships
  - Lead to ISP blacklisting of Zeta's sending infrastructure
  - Result in financial losses due to poor email deliverability

Monitoring and maintaining domain reputation is critical to ensuring campaign
success, client satisfaction, and protecting Zeta's sender infrastructure.

SECTION 2: THE MULTI-AGENT AI SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Architecture Overview
---------------------
The Domain Reputation AI uses a multi-agent architecture with three specialized
AI agents working in sequence:

  Agent 1: CLASSIFIER AGENT
  --------------------------
  Responsibility: Scores each email metric individually (0-100 scale) and
  calculates a weighted overall reputation score.

  Input:  Raw email metrics (delivery rate, open rate, etc.)
  Output: Overall score (0-100), per-metric scores, classification label
          (GOOD / AVERAGE / BAD)

  Agent 2: DIAGNOSIS AGENT
  -------------------------
  Responsibility: Identifies root causes of reputation problems by analyzing
  each metric against thresholds and generating structured issue reports.

  Input:  Raw metrics + thresholds
  Output: List of issues with severity (critical/warning/info), affected metric,
          description, and supporting detail

  Agent 3: RECOMMENDATION AGENT
  ------------------------------
  Responsibility: Generates a prioritized action plan based on the diagnosed
  issues, ranked by impact and severity.

  Input:  Diagnosed issues list
  Output: Ranked list of recommendations with impact level, effort required,
          and specific action steps

Orchestrator
------------
The Orchestrator coordinates all three agents in sequence:
  Step 1 → Fetch domain metrics from data source
  Step 2 → Classifier Agent scores and classifies
  Step 3 → Diagnosis Agent identifies issues
  Step 4 → Recommendation Agent generates action plan
  Step 5 → Return complete analysis report

SECTION 3: EMAIL METRICS EXPLAINED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The system tracks and analyzes 7 core email performance metrics.
Each metric is scored individually and contributes to the overall reputation score.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 1: DELIVERY RATE
Weight in Overall Score: 20%
Direction: Higher is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of sent emails that were successfully delivered to the
  recipient's mailbox (not bounced or blocked).

  Delivery Rate = (Emails Delivered / Emails Sent) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 95% or above
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 90% to 94.9%
  ❌ BAD     : Below 90%

What low delivery rate means:
  - ISPs may be blocking emails from the domain
  - Domain or sending IP may be blacklisted
  - SPF, DKIM, or DMARC authentication may be misconfigured
  - High bounce rates are causing ISPs to throttle or reject emails

Impact: HIGH — directly affects whether emails reach recipients at all.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 2: OPEN RATE
Weight in Overall Score: 20%
Direction: Higher is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of delivered emails that were opened by recipients.
  Includes both human opens and automated/bot opens.

  Open Rate = (Total Opens / Emails Delivered) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 20% or above
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 10% to 19.9%
  ❌ BAD     : Below 10%

What low open rate means:
  - Subject lines are not compelling or relevant
  - Emails may be landing in spam folder (even if "delivered")
  - Sender name is not recognized or trusted by recipients
  - Recipient list is outdated or unengaged
  - Wrong send time — emails arriving when recipients are least active

Impact: HIGH — open rate reflects both deliverability and recipient trust.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 3: GENUINE OPEN RATE
Weight in Overall Score: 15%
Direction: Higher is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of delivered emails opened by actual human recipients,
  excluding automated bot opens (Apple MPP, security scanners, etc.).

  Genuine Open Rate = (Human Opens / Emails Delivered) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 15% or above
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 8% to 14.9%
  ❌ BAD     : Below 8%

Why this matters:
  Since Apple's Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) launched in 2021, automated
  systems pre-fetch email content which registers false opens. Genuine open
  rate strips these out to show real human engagement.

What low genuine open rate means:
  - Most opens are bot-generated (inflated vanity metrics)
  - Real recipients are not engaging with emails
  - List quality may be poor — addresses may belong to inactive or
    non-existent users

Impact: MEDIUM-HIGH — indicates true recipient engagement level.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 4: CLICK RATE
Weight in Overall Score: 10%
Direction: Higher is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of delivered emails where at least one link was clicked
  by the recipient.

  Click Rate = (Emails with Clicks / Emails Delivered) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 2% or above
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 1% to 1.9%
  ❌ BAD     : Below 1%

What low click rate means:
  - Email content is not relevant to the audience
  - Calls-to-action (CTAs) are unclear, hard to find, or unconvincing
  - Landing page mismatch — recipients click but do not convert
  - Email design may not be rendering correctly on mobile devices

Impact: MEDIUM — reflects content quality and campaign effectiveness.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 5: ABUSE RATE (SPAM COMPLAINT RATE)
Weight in Overall Score: 20%
Direction: Lower is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of delivered emails marked as spam or junk by recipients.
  ISPs report this via Feedback Loop (FBL) programs.

  Abuse Rate = (Spam Complaints / Emails Delivered) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 0.1% or below
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 0.1% to 0.3%
  ❌ BAD     : Above 0.3%

Industry Standard:
  Google and Yahoo require abuse rates below 0.3% to maintain deliverability.
  Exceeding 0.1% consistently triggers ISP monitoring.

What high abuse rate means:
  - Recipients did not consent to receive emails (poor opt-in process)
  - Unsubscribe link is difficult to find or not functioning
  - Email frequency is too high — recipients prefer to mark spam
    rather than unsubscribe
  - Content is irrelevant or misleading

CRITICAL: Above 0.3% abuse rate can result in immediate ISP blocking
and domain blacklisting. This is the highest-risk metric.

Impact: CRITICAL — directly triggers ISP penalties and blacklisting.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 6: HARD BOUNCE RATE (HARD ERROR RATE)
Weight in Overall Score: 10%
Direction: Lower is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of sent emails that permanently failed to deliver due to
  invalid, non-existent, or blocked email addresses.

  Hard Bounce Rate = (Hard Bounces / Emails Sent) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 2% or below
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 2% to 5%
  ❌ BAD     : Above 5%

Difference between Hard and Soft Bounce:
  Hard Bounce = Permanent failure (address doesn't exist, domain invalid)
  Soft Bounce = Temporary failure (mailbox full, server temporarily unavailable)

What high hard bounce rate means:
  - Email list contains old, invalid, or purchased addresses
  - No email validation at point of data capture
  - Lists have not been cleaned or updated recently
  - Suppression lists are not being properly applied

Impact: HIGH — ISPs view repeated delivery attempts to invalid addresses
as a spam signal and will throttle or block the sending domain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRIC 7: UNSUBSCRIBE RATE
Weight in Overall Score: 5%
Direction: Lower is better
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Definition:
  The percentage of delivered emails that resulted in the recipient clicking
  the unsubscribe link.

  Unsub Rate = (Unsubscribes / Emails Delivered) × 100

Thresholds:
  ✅ GOOD    : 0.5% or below
  ⚠️ AVERAGE : 0.5% to 1%
  ❌ BAD     : Above 1%

What high unsub rate means:
  - Email content does not match what recipients signed up for
  - Sending frequency is too high
  - List segmentation is poor — wrong audience receiving wrong content
  - Emails are not personalized enough

Note: A high unsub rate is less dangerous than a high abuse rate, but
persistently high unsub rates indicate audience misalignment and will
erode list quality over time.

Impact: LOW-MEDIUM — indicator of content-audience mismatch.

SECTION 4: SCORING SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

How Individual Metric Scores Are Calculated (0-100)
----------------------------------------------------

Each metric is scored on a 0-100 scale:

For metrics where HIGHER is better (delivery rate, open rate, genuine open rate,
click rate):

  If value >= GOOD threshold  → Score = 100
  If value >= AVG threshold   → Score = 50 + 50 × (value - AVG) / (GOOD - AVG)
  If value < AVG threshold    → Score = max(0, 50 × value / AVG)

For metrics where LOWER is better (abuse rate, hard bounce rate, unsub rate):

  If value <= GOOD threshold  → Score = 100
  If value <= AVG threshold   → Score = 50 + 50 × (AVG - value) / (AVG - GOOD)
  If value > AVG threshold    → Score = max(0, 50 × AVG / value)

Metric Weights
--------------
  Delivery Rate       20%  (0.20)
  Open Rate           20%  (0.20)
  Abuse Rate          20%  (0.20)
  Genuine Open Rate   15%  (0.15)
  Click Rate          10%  (0.10)
  Hard Bounce Rate    10%  (0.10)
  Unsubscribe Rate     5%  (0.05)
  ─────────────────────────────
  TOTAL              100%  (1.00)

Overall Reputation Score
------------------------
  Overall Score = Σ (Metric Score × Metric Weight) for all 7 metrics

  Result: A single score from 0 to 100

SECTION 5: CLASSIFICATION LABELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Based on the Overall Score, each domain receives one of three labels:

  ✅ GOOD    — Score 70 to 100
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The domain has strong email sending reputation. Emails are reaching
  inboxes reliably. Engagement metrics are healthy. No immediate action
  required, but continued monitoring is recommended.

  ⚠️ AVERAGE — Score 40 to 69
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The domain has moderate reputation with some areas of concern.
  Certain metrics are underperforming and could deteriorate further
  without attention. Action should be taken within 1-2 weeks to
  prevent decline into BAD status.

  ❌ BAD     — Score 0 to 39
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The domain has poor reputation. Multiple critical metrics are
  underperforming. ISP blocks, blacklisting, or spam folder placement
  is likely occurring. Immediate remediation action is required.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: ISSUE SEVERITY LEVELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Diagnosis Agent classifies each identified problem into one of three
severity levels:

  🔴 CRITICAL
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Requires immediate action. The metric has breached a critical threshold
  that puts the domain at risk of blacklisting, ISP blocking, or severe
  deliverability loss.

  Examples:
  - Delivery rate below 90%
  - Open rate below 10%
  - Abuse rate above 0.3%
  - Hard bounce rate above 5%

  🟠 WARNING
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Requires action within 1-2 weeks. The metric is underperforming but
  has not yet reached a critical level. If unaddressed, it will likely
  deteriorate to CRITICAL status.

  Examples:
  - Delivery rate between 90-95%
  - Open rate between 10-20%
  - Abuse rate between 0.1-0.3%

  🔵 INFO
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Informational alert. The metric is slightly below target but is not
  causing significant harm. Monitoring and gradual improvement is advised.

  Examples:
  - Click rate slightly below average
  - Unsubscribe rate slightly elevated

SECTION 7: DIAGNOSIS — ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Diagnosis Agent evaluates each metric and generates specific root cause
explanations when thresholds are breached.

DELIVERY RATE ISSUES
─────────────────────
Critical (< 90%):
  "Critically low delivery rate — most emails not reaching inboxes"
  Detail: Less than 90% of sent emails are being delivered. This signals ISP
  blocks, IP blacklisting, or domain reputation damage.

Warning (90-95%):
  "Below-average delivery rate — inbox placement needs attention"
  Detail: Delivery rate is between 90–95%. Minor ISP filtering or list quality
  issues may be present.

OPEN RATE ISSUES
─────────────────
Critical (< 10%):
  "Very low open rate — recipients not engaging with emails"
  Detail: Under 10% open rate indicates poor sender reputation, spam folder
  placement, or unengaged list.

Warning (10-20%):
  "Below-average open rate — subject lines or sender reputation may be weak"
  Detail: Open rate between 10–20%. Room to improve via better subject lines
  and send-time optimization.

GENUINE OPEN RATE ISSUES
─────────────────────────
Critical (< 8%):
  "Very low genuine (human) open rate — bot opens masking real engagement"
  Detail: Genuine opens under 8% suggest the list has poor human engagement.
  Bot-inflated opens may hide deeper reputation issues.

Warning (8-15%):
  "Genuine open rate is below target — actual human engagement is limited"
  Detail: Genuine opens between 8–15%. Consider list hygiene and
  re-engagement campaigns.

ABUSE RATE ISSUES
─────────────────
Critical (> 0.3%):
  "High spam complaint (abuse) rate — serious reputation risk"
  Detail: Abuse rate above 0.3% triggers ISP reputation penalties and can
  lead to domain blacklisting.

Warning (0.1-0.3%):
  "Elevated spam complaint rate — approaching ISP threshold"
  Detail: Abuse rate between 0.1–0.3%. Monitor closely — exceeding 0.3%
  causes ISP throttling.

HARD BOUNCE RATE ISSUES
────────────────────────
Critical (> 5%):
  "High hard bounce rate — large volume of invalid addresses"
  Detail: Hard bounce rate above 5% indicates a severely outdated or
  low-quality list. ISPs penalize domains for repeated delivery attempts
  to invalid addresses.

Warning (2-5%):
  "Elevated hard bounce rate — list contains stale or invalid addresses"
  Detail: Hard bounce rate between 2–5%. Regular list cleaning and
  suppression updates are needed.

CLICK RATE ISSUES
─────────────────
Warning (< 1%):
  "Very low click-through rate — email content not driving action"
  Detail: Click rate under 1% suggests content relevance issues or poor
  placement of CTAs.

Info (1-2%):
  "Below-average click rate — call-to-action effectiveness can be improved"
  Detail: Click rate between 1–2%. A/B testing subject lines and CTAs
  could improve this.

UNSUBSCRIBE RATE ISSUES
────────────────────────
Warning (> 1%):
  "High unsubscribe rate — recipients opting out at an elevated pace"
  Detail: Unsub rate above 1% indicates content-audience mismatch
  or over-mailing.

Info (0.5-1%):
  "Slightly elevated unsubscribe rate"
  Detail: Review content personalization and mailing cadence.

SECTION 8: RECOMMENDATIONS BY ISSUE TYPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DELIVERY RATE — CRITICAL ACTIONS
──────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Check domain and IP blacklist status immediately (MXToolbox, Talos)
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Review ISP feedback loops and bounce logs for block patterns
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

  Priority 3: Audit authentication records — SPF, DKIM, DMARC alignment
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

  Priority 4: Consider IP warm-up on a dedicated sending IP
              Impact: High | Effort: High

DELIVERY RATE — WARNING ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Verify SPF and DKIM records are correctly published
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Reduce sending volume temporarily to recover sender reputation
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 3: Review PMTA bounce handling and retry policies
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium

OPEN RATE — CRITICAL ACTIONS
──────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Run A/B tests on subject lines — personalize with recipient's name
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Segment list and send only to most engaged users first
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

  Priority 3: Optimize send time — test morning vs evening delivery windows
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 4: Remove cold subscribers (no opens in last 90 days)
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

OPEN RATE — WARNING ACTIONS
─────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Test emoji or question-based subject lines to boost open rates
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Review preheader text — it displays next to subject in inbox
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

ABUSE RATE — CRITICAL ACTIONS
───────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Immediately suppress all complainers and honor feedback loop data
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Audit consent — ensure all recipients explicitly opted in
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

  Priority 3: Review sending frequency — reduce to 1–2 times per week max
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

  Priority 4: Make unsubscribe link prominent — reduce friction to opt out
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

HARD BOUNCE RATE — CRITICAL ACTIONS
─────────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Run full list validation — remove all invalid and non-existent emails
              Impact: High | Effort: Medium

  Priority 2: Enforce real-time email verification at point of data capture
              Impact: High | Effort: High

  Priority 3: Suppress all hard bounces immediately — stop retrying invalid emails
              Impact: High | Effort: Low

CLICK RATE — IMPROVEMENT ACTIONS
──────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Redesign CTA buttons — use action-oriented text and prominent placement
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Improve email content relevance through audience segmentation
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium

  Priority 3: Test single-CTA emails vs multi-link layout
              Impact: Low | Effort: Low

UNSUBSCRIBE RATE — IMPROVEMENT ACTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1: Reduce sending frequency for high-volume campaigns
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Low

  Priority 2: Introduce email preference center — let users choose frequency
              Impact: Medium | Effort: Medium

  Priority 3: Survey unsubscribers to understand exit reasons
              Impact: Low | Effort: Low

SECTION 11: GLOSSARY OF TERMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Abuse Rate       — Percentage of emails marked as spam by recipients
Bounce           — Email that could not be delivered to recipient's inbox
DKIM             — DomainKeys Identified Mail; email authentication method
DMARC            — Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance
Delivery Rate    — Percentage of sent emails successfully delivered
Domain           — The part of an email address after the @ symbol (e.g., zeta.com)
FBL              — Feedback Loop; ISP mechanism to report spam complaints
Genuine Open     — Email open confirmed to be from a human, not a bot
Hard Bounce      — Permanent delivery failure (invalid address)
ISP              — Internet Service Provider (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, etc.)
MPP              — Apple Mail Privacy Protection (causes inflated open rates)
Open Rate        — Percentage of delivered emails that were opened
Reputation       — ISP's trust score for a sending domain
Soft Bounce      — Temporary delivery failure (full mailbox, server down)
SPF              — Sender Policy Framework; email authentication standard
Suppression List — List of addresses to never email (unsubscribes, bounces)
Unsubscribe Rate — Percentage of recipients who opted out of emails
